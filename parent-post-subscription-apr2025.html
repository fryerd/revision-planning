How parents come to buy Seneca Premium

A full-year read of 626 parents who upgraded their child's Seneca account between April 2025 and April 2026 — told largely in their own words. This report traces the parent pathway to Premium: where they first hear about it, what triggers the purchase, which package they choose, and where they hesitate along the way.

626 parent responses 12 months of continuous fielding 6-question in-product survey First clean parent year since the routing fix

The one-minute takeaway

Parent conversion is not cold acquisition — it's a warm-hand-off from the child or from school. Roughly half of parents first hear about Seneca through their child's school; another big share through the child themselves. Once they find out, they convert with conviction — 44% report no hesitation at all. Seven-in-ten buy Exam Boost (the middle tier); Amelia AI Assistant takes a remarkable 52% of parent purchases in Autumn 2025, falling back to 20–37% in the rest of the year. Awareness is the single biggest barrier to earlier purchase — parents who don't know Premium exists simply don't buy it. When they do know, they buy.

The awareness ceiling

Across ~70 substantive "why not sooner?" answers, roughly one in three parents say they simply didn't know Seneca Premium existed. They aren't rejecting a product — they're discovering one. The single biggest lever on parent conversion isn't price or feature set; it's making sure parents hear about Premium earlier. Every warm-hand-off channel — school newsletters, parent emails, the child themselves — converts parents who, once told, convert.

Which package parents buy

Seneca Premium shows parents a three-option page: Exam Boost (Seneca's middle tier), Amelia AI Assistant (the premium AI tutor), and a 1:1 tutoring partner product. Parents don't see Supreme — the School-Premium lookup is too slow on the parent flow to safely offer it. The mix is stable except for one feature: Amelia's autumn surge.

Package choice by period

Package P1 Exam '25 P2 Autumn '25 P3 Spring '26
Exam Boost · middle tier 71% 62% 72%
Amelia AI Assistant · premium AI upgrade 33% 52% 37%

Exam Boost remains dominant every period. Amelia, however, hit 52% of parent purchases in Autumn 2025 — the highest AI-tier uptake ever recorded in the parent post-subscription surveys. The previous autumn high was 40% (Autumn 2023, when Amelia was first locked in as the top tier). Two plausible drivers: parents more comfortable with the "AI tutor" category post-ChatGPT, and Amelia itself running on materially better LLMs than its 2023 davinci-era launch.

Opportunities and threats for the parent-funded B2C business

Read across a full year, the parent voice points at concrete levers — and at one competitive threat that has emerged for the first time.

Opportunities

Opportunity · Marketing

Close the awareness gap

One in three parents who eventually buy say they didn't know Seneca Premium existed until recently. School partnerships, parent-direct email, and SEN coordinator outreach all convert when they reach a new parent. This is the single highest-leverage growth lever in the parent dataset.

Opportunity · Commercial NB: shipped Feb 2026

Monthly subscription

The most-repeated hesitation in every parent post-subscription survey since October 2022. Shipped in February 2026 after a year of 10% testing. The watching-brief now is monthly-vs-seasonal pricing perception and whether the "advertised as monthly, quarterly at checkout" confusion has been resolved.

Opportunity · Product

Lean into the "cheaper than a private tutor" frame

Multiple parents explicitly position Amelia as a tutoring substitute — *"instead of private tutor"*, *"like asking a teacher"*. With private tutors at £30–40/hour, a sharper cost-comparison narrative on the Amelia upgrade page could grow share.

Opportunity · Product

Parent-as-teacher functionality

Repeated ask across parent surveys: *"I actually want to set them the whole course and watch them work through it."* Parents want to assign, track, and nudge — not just view a report. A light parent-dashboard with assignment capability would address a real, named gap.

Opportunity · Positioning

Keep investing in the SEN / home-ed / struggling-child narrative

A meaningful, emotionally-loaded share of parent buyers are supporting a child with ADHD, autism, anxiety, home education, or school attendance difficulties. Seneca Premium is serving them well — and the parent voice is grateful.

Opportunity · Commercial

Family / multi-child plans

Small-count but recurring — parents with multiple children explicitly say they'd pay for a second if the price-per-child was lower. Quote: *"I have two children... I can't justify a £100 outlay."* Scoping is not complex; the demand is documented.

Threats

Threat · Competitive

ChatGPT as an Amelia substitute

First explicit "why pay for an AI tutor when ChatGPT is free?" quotes appeared this year on the student side. Amelia's differentiation — curriculum awareness, safety, progress integration — needs clearer positioning before the pattern scales to parents.

Threat · Trust

Pricing-comms transparency

Several parents used "misleading" in their hesitation free text — the price they saw in marketing or early in the flow didn't match the price at checkout. Especially relevant now that monthly is live alongside seasonal and annual. Fixable with comms, not product.

Threat · Experience

Upsell-pressure fatigue

Quote: *"Multiple prompts to buy into more felt not nice."* A small number of parents describe the purchase experience as over-nudgy. Worth a UX audit — parents converting despite friction is the kind of signal that erodes Trustpilot scores over time.

Threat · Attribution

AI-recommendation discovery is a double-edged channel

Gemini and similar AI tools are now an emerging discovery channel. Good news: it's converting. Risk: AI search indexing is volatile, and Seneca's appearance inside AI recommendations isn't something the business controls directly. Worth monitoring.
