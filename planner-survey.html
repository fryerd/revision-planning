03 — What Students Like

Organisation is the killer feature — but motivation matters too

109 students wrote about what they like. Five themes emerged, all confirming or extending the interview findings.

📋

Organisation & structure

66% of responses

Removes the cognitive burden of deciding what to study, when, and for how long. The dominant positive theme by a wide margin.

“It organises my revision when I don’t know what to do.”

Smart subject distribution

26% of responses

Students notice and value that the planner allocates more time to subjects they’re less confident in. A key differentiator vs. generic AI scheduling.

“It evenly spaces out my subjects so I don’t revise one more than the other, but it also prioritises subjects I’m not confident with.”

90-minute cap is the #1 frustration

32% of pain point responses

Students want 2–5 hours per day, especially during half term, Easter, and study leave. The cap makes the planner cover only a fraction of their day.

“90 minutes is very easy to do, so I would like an option to do it for 400 minutes please?”
Y11 · R:5
🔒

Plan editing is too rigid

24% of pain point responses

Can’t swap subjects, adjust session lengths, or modify without restarting. Students want daily override capability for tests and tutors.

“No ability to edit length of sessions, add a new session, or delete session when creating plan. Also you can’t edit the current plan without starting again.”
Y11 · R:3
📅

Can’t see future days

15% — new at scale

Students only see today’s tasks. They want a weekly view to plan around commitments. Not raised in interviews — surfaces through daily use.

“I cannot quickly view the entire weekly plan day by day; there should be a calendar view.”

05 — Prioritised Actions

What to build next

Recommendations ordered by frequency of student demand, severity of impact, and convergence with interview findings.

P0 — Do now
01
High Impact

Raise the daily time cap to 3–4+ hours, particularly during holidays

The #1 pain point (32%) and improvement request (28%). The 90-minute limit makes the planner inadequate for serious revision. Students want 2–5 hours, especially during half term, Easter, and study leave. However, this needs careful design — a higher default cap could disincentivise less engaged or less committed students by making the daily target feel unachievable. Consider keeping a sensible term-time default but allowing students to raise their own cap, or offering a “revision mode” toggle for intensive periods.

32% of pain points 28% of improvement requests Confirmed by interviews
02
High Impact

Add daily subject swap / override

Students can’t change what they study on a given day. When they have a test tomorrow or a tutor session, the plan becomes irrelevant. Allow moving tasks between days without resetting.

20% of improvement requests 4/7 interview students Abandonment risk
03
High Impact

Allow custom exam / mock date input

The fixed May 2026 endpoint is wrong for Year 10 (April mocks) and Year 13 (June exams). Let students set their own end date during planner setup.

13% of pain points 5/7 interview students Credibility issue
P1 — Do next
04
Medium Impact

Add a weekly overview / calendar view

Students can only see today’s tasks. A simple weekly view showing daily assignments would address a new-at-scale pain point (15%). Not raised in interviews — surfaced through real daily use.

15% of pain points 11% of improvement requests New at scale
05
Medium Impact

Enable in-place plan editing

Allow students to adjust session lengths, add/remove sessions, and modify the plan without starting over. Currently, any change requires a full restart.

16% of improvement requests Extends interview Theme 2
06
Medium Impact

Fix time-tracking accuracy

Students report completed sessions showing as incomplete, and recorded time not matching actual study time. A trust issue that erodes confidence in the system.

9% of pain points New (technical)
P2 — Consider
07
Explore

Topic-level revision control, notifications, streaks & progress

Longer-term improvements: let students pick specific topics (not just subjects); add push/email reminders; add streaks and weekly progress reports. Each requested by 6–9% of respondents.

06 — Interview Convergence

Survey confirms the top 3 interview priorities — and adds new findings

Comparing 126 survey responses against 13 themes from 8 student interviews (Feb 2026).

Confirmed

Core value proposition (Theme 1)

66% cite organisation as top strength. Interviews: 7/7 students positive. The planner solves a real, felt problem — now validated at scale.

Confirmed

Day-level flexibility (Theme 2)

24% cite editing rigidity. Interviews: 4/7 requested this. Survey adds session-length editing and plan deletion as sub-needs.

Confirmed

Custom exam dates (Theme 3)

13% cite wrong dates. Interviews: 5/7 raised this. Both Y10 (April mocks) and Y13 (June A-levels) affected.

Confirmed & escalated

90-minute cap (Theme 7)

32% cite as #1 pain point. Interviews: only 2/7 raised this. Survey reveals this is the single most urgent fix — not a niche concern.

New at scale

No weekly view

15% want to see future days. Not raised in interviews. This pain point only surfaces through daily real-world use, not walkthrough sessions.

New (technical)

Timer inaccuracies

9% report time-tracking bugs. Not raised in interviews. Requires sustained use to notice — a trust issue that needs investigation.

Confirmed

Notifications (Theme 5)

8% request reminders. Interviews: 3/7 requested this. Consistent signal across both methods.

New

External platform integration

4% want to block time for Sparx, Tassomai, Duolingo. Students revise across multiple platforms and want one central plan.
