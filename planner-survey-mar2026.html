05 — Deeper Findings

What the numbers alone don’t tell you

Eight patterns that surfaced from reading every response — non-obvious insights that keyword analysis alone cannot find.

💪

The planner rewards sustained use

Students who created their plan “a few weeks ago” rate 4.02/5. “Today” users rate 3.80. The planner gets better with time, but first-use friction (wrong dates, no weekly view) risks losing students before value compounds.

It’s really helped with just concentrating on what I need to revise and reducing the stress of the magnitude of content.
Year 11 — established user
💥

“Missed day” is the silent killer

At 45%, the #1 pain point. One missed day permanently breaks the week. Students want to redistribute missed time, not skip it. The planner treats missed days as sunk; students treat them as debt to repay.

When we are busy one day and don’t complete the aloted time for a subject we can’t make up the time and get it to fill in the weekly time
Year 11
☀️

90-min cap and holiday mode are the same problem

40% want more than 90 mins. 53% want a holiday planner. Both stem from the same root: the planner assumes term-time light revision. Students approaching exams need 3–6 hours across multiple subjects.

I have my Easter holidays coming up for three weeks and it would be good to be able to plan more than 90m each day
Year 11
🎯

Students want one source of truth

8 students want to log non-Seneca revision (textbooks, flashcards, Sparx Maths). 24% say subjects are missing. When the planner can’t include all subjects, students build parallel systems and the planner becomes partial.

Let me put in subjects that I can’t revise on Seneca, so I can have Economics scheduled but tick it off instead of doing it on the app.
Year 11
🔒

“Can’t delete” is a consent problem

9 students want to delete their planner. 3 rated 1/5. The intensity is disproportionate to the count. Students feel trapped in a commitment they didn’t understand. Every persistent feature needs an off-ramp.

Every time I try to delete my planner by not adding any subjects, it doesn’t work, even if I try to reset the page. I wished there is a button that can let me delete my planners.
Year 11
🔢

Fractional times feel broken

Session lengths like “59 and a third minutes” or “27 minutes instead of 30” feel arbitrary. Students think in round numbers. Rounding to the nearest 5 minutes would feel more natural.

It keeps changing my timings to random numbers — 27 minutes instead of 30 minutes doesn’t work as well. 30 minute blocks are easier to plan into a day.
Year 10
🔔

The planner is generating demand for notifications

46% want reminder notifications. The planner creates a schedule but doesn’t prompt students to follow it. With Easter approaching and 150 students requesting this, the timing argument has strengthened significantly.

I really need reminder notifications — remind me that I have to finish my planned studies for the day
Year 11
📄

Plan view duplicates “For You”

One student explicitly noted: “the today’s plan study recommendations are 100% the same as For You.” If the planner’s daily view duplicates existing surfaces, it isn’t adding differentiated value.

The today’s plan study recommendations are 100% the same as “For You”, you may as well be on any as they show same stuff.
Year 11
06 — Priorities

What to build, and in what order

Ranked by frequency, severity, and time-sensitivity. P0 items should ship before Easter break; P1 within 4–8 weeks.

P0 — Do now (before Easter)
01
High

Add missed-day catch-up

When a student misses a day, redistribute missed revision across remaining days in the week. The #1 pain point at 45%. One missed day should not break the entire week — this is where abandonment happens.

45% — #1 pain point Abandonment driver
I would love to be able to see the subjects I missed on a day and be able to do its time on another day so that I still spend the amount of time I set myself
Year 11
02
High

Add a holiday / intensive revision mode

Raise the daily time cap for holidays to at least 3–4 hours. Allow more subjects per day. 53% requested this, and Easter break starts in weeks. Keep the current cap as a term-time default; offer an “intensive mode” toggle for holidays.

53% want holiday mode 40% want >90 mins 81 free-text mentions
I think it is different for everybody and their goals but for me personally maybe 6h per day during the holidays
Year 11
03
High

Allow custom exam / mock dates

59% want this. Y10 students with April mocks and Y13 students with staggered A-level dates are actively harmed by the default endpoint. Let students set their own end date during onboarding.

59% improvement request 35% pain point
I am in Y10, so I would like to make the exam adjustments better
Year 10
P1 — Do next (4–8 weeks)
04
Medium

Implement push notifications

46% want reminders at their scheduled study time. The planner creates a schedule but doesn’t prompt students to follow it. The gap between having a plan and following it is where notifications live.

46% improvement request Also in interviews
Reminders for homework deadlines, upcoming tests, and revision sessions would be most helpful, with notifications both in advance and on the day so nothing is missed
Year 10
05
Medium

Add a weekly overview view

30% can’t see what’s coming up. Students want to see tomorrow, the rest of the week, and plan around commitments. A simple calendar showing daily subject assignments would address this.

30% pain point Frustration, not blocking
I cannot quickly view the entire weekly plan day by day; there should be a calendar view
Year 11
06
Medium

Add daily subject swap

39% say the daily schedule isn’t flexible enough. Let students choose which subject to study on a given day without resetting the plan. Real-world conflicts (tutors, school tests) need daily flex.

39% pain point Rigidity perception
I don’t like how we can’t change which subject/s we are doing for the day as I might have a surprise test
Year 11
P2 — Consider
07
Explore

Streaks, rewards, and progress reports

65% selected this — the highest of any improvement option. Design streaks that reward consistency without punishing missed days. Weekly summaries showing time completed vs. planned would close the feedback loop.

65% — most-requested
Unlocking some cute characters ranked from common to secret...

Let students log revision done outside Seneca. A simple timer + “mark as done” feature would make the planner the single source of truth for all revision activity, not just Seneca content.
