05 — Deeper Findings

What the data tells us beyond the numbers

⏰

The 90-minute cap is the new holiday mode

9% raised the daily time cap as a pain point — same intensity as Wave 2. With Easter live and exams approaching, students want 2–4 hours per day.

“I wish there was more than 90 mins… I have a lot of work to do especially with my exams coming up.”
Year 11
📚

Students treat the planner as their entire revision system

Students use the planner for all subjects but don’t exclusively use Seneca content. They want to log revision done outside Seneca and mark it complete.

“I have five subjects on my planner and I don’t really use Seneca for English. I enjoy doing past papers but there’s no way to tick off revision.”
Year 11, rated 4/5
📅

Y10 students have a fundamentally different problem

37% of respondents are Year 10. The planner defaults to May 2026 exams, but Y10 mocks are weeks earlier and actual GCSEs are May 2027. This makes the planner either too relaxed or targeting the wrong date.

“It keeps saying my exams are in May 2026 when I am in Year 10 — it only gives a revision programme up to then.”
Year 10, rated 4/5
📚

“New topics” creates tension with school pacing

Students interpret “new” as “content my teacher hasn’t taught yet.” This creates anxiety for mock students and irrelevance for students mid-term.

“The new topic section isn’t very useful… It means that you are not revising the topic but learning it instead.”
Year 12, rated 4/5
🔒

Premium paywall in free user flows breaks the contract

When the planner recommends wrong answer mode and the student can’t access it, the system sets expectations it can’t fulfil. Students who complete all sections have nothing left to do.

“Wrong answer mode is a bit stupid because you can’t use it unless you upgrade to premium.”
Year 11, rated 3/5
👉

Students expect recommendations to be interactive

Multiple students tried to click on recommendation cards expecting navigation to that activity. The recommendations appear display-only, but students expect them to be actionable entry points.

“I wanted to be able to click on the ideas and do them but it didn’t work… take me to a separate page based on whatever it said I should do.”
Year 10, rated 3/5

What’s changed

The pain point landscape has fragmented. Wave 2 had a single dominant issue (missed days at 45%); Wave 3 has many smaller issues — no single theme exceeds 13%. The missed-day catch-up feature has effectively resolved the #1 Wave 2 complaint. The conversation has shifted from “what’s missing” to “how can the recommendations be smarter.”

What persists

The 90-minute daily cap (9% in both waves), exam date inflexibility (down from 35% to 8% but still present), and the weekly view request (13%) are recurring themes. Spaced repetition is validated. The new frontier is topic relevance — students want recommendations that respect what they’ve actually been taught.
